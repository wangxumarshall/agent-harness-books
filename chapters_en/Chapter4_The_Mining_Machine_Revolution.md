## Prologue: The Awakening of a Genius (2011–2012)

### I. The Flash of the Youth Class at USTC

Hefei, 2011. The summer sun blazed with ferocious heat. In a dormitory room of the Special Class for the Gifted Young at the University of Science and Technology of China, an old desktop computer hummed softly, its blue screen glow illuminating a young, slender face.

Jiang Xinyu stared at the numbers dancing across the screen, his fingers drumming unconsciously on the desk.

"Bitcoin……" he murmured the name, his voice hoarse.

As a prodigy of the Special Class for the Gifted Young at USTC, Jiang Xinyu had long been accustomed to thinking differently from ordinary people. While his peers were sweating over final exams, he had already completed his undergraduate coursework in computer science. When his classmates began pursuing master's degrees, he had already received an admission offer from Yale University's PhD program. In everyone's eyes, he was an undisputed genius—the boy who could solve complex differential equations in three minutes, the prodigy admitted to a top university at seventeen.

But at this moment, the concept of this decentralized digital currency on the screen filled this widely acknowledged genius with an unprecedented tremor.

"No central bank, no government backing, value maintained entirely through cryptographic principles and a distributed network……" Jiang Xinyu pushed his black-framed glasses up the bridge of his nose. "This is truly a perfect crystallization of mathematics and cryptography."

He stood up and paced the cramped dormitory room. In the six-square-meter space, a bed, a desk, and a computer occupied nearly all the room. But his mind had long since soared beyond this confined physical space, entering an entirely new dimension.

He returned to the computer, opened the Bitcoin forum, and began reading every technical document with ravenous hunger.

He had studied Satoshi Nakamoto's whitepaper no fewer than ten times, and each reading revealed new details. Those seemingly dry mathematical formulas and technical descriptions were as beautiful as poetry in his eyes.

"Proof of work…… hash computation…… blockchain……" These terms wove together in his mind into a grand vision.

Suddenly, an idea struck him like lightning.

Jiang Xinyu leapt to his feet, his knee slamming into the corner of the desk, but he felt nothing. His heart raced; blood surged through his veins.

"If we could design a chip specifically for Secure Hash Algorithm 256-bit (SHA-256) computation, wouldn't mining efficiency increase by hundreds or even thousands of times?"

At the time, Bitcoin mining was still停留在 the era of Central Processing Units (CPU), with Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) mining only just emerging. Some Field-Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) miners already existed on the market, but the efficiency gains were limited. Most people believed GPU mining was already sufficiently efficient, with little room for further optimization.

But Jiang Xinyu saw a much farther vision—Application-Specific Integrated Circuits (ASIC) would completely revolutionize this industry. ASICs are chips designed for specific applications. Unlike general-purpose processors, they could be极致 optimized for particular algorithms, achieving performance and energy efficiency levels that general-purpose processors could never reach.

"A CPU is a general-purpose computing device, designed to handle all manner of tasks, so its efficiency on any specific algorithm is necessarily limited." He spoke to himself, his voice trembling with excitement. "While GPUs have strong parallel processing capabilities, they are fundamentally designed for graphics rendering—a lot of transistors are wasted on functions unrelated to mining."

He walked to the window and pushed it open, letting the heat flood into the room. In the distance, Hefei's nightscape was unfolding, ten thousand lights glittering like stars on the ground.

"Only ASICs," he continued. "Only chips optimized specifically for the SHA-256 algorithm can unleash the greatest computational potential. If we could design a chip that devotes every single transistor to hash computation, the efficiency would be hundreds or thousands of times that of GPUs!"

The breathtaking nature of this idea lay not just in its potential to transform the technical landscape of Bitcoin mining, but in its power to reshape the entire industry's power structure. Whoever controlled the most advanced mining technology would control the Bitcoin network's hashrate, and thus control the话语权.

Jiang Xinyu returned to the computer and opened a blank document, beginning to sketch out the technical blueprint for an ASIC miner.

"The project codename……" He paused, his gaze falling on a stuffed toy cat in the corner of the laboratory—a memento left by his ex-girlfriend, a chubby orange kitten that had earned the name "Friedcat" because he often held it while thinking.

Friedcat. 烤猫.

The setting sun painted the sky blood-red beyond the window. In that ordinary dormitory room, a plan that would soon shock the entire cryptocurrency world was quietly taking shape.

### IV. Days and Nights of R&D

Chip design is an extraordinarily complex and precise endeavor, requiring teams to work tirelessly. It is like building a city in a space the size of a fingernail—every transistor, every wire must be designed with pinpoint accuracy.

Jiang Xinyu assembled a lean, capable technical team, most members coming from top domestic chip design companies and research institutes. They rented a modest office in Nanshan District, Shenzhen, and began the arduous work of research and development.

"The chip architecture has been finalized," Jiang Xinyu stood before a whiteboard, explaining the design plan to team members. "We are using a multi-core parallel architecture, with each chip integrating 48 SHA-256 computing cores, interconnected via a bus and sharing a memory controller."

"Each core is an independent hash computation unit, capable of processing different nonce values in parallel. The benefit of this design is flexibility—we can selectively enable or disable certain cores as needed to achieve the optimal balance between power consumption and performance."

"Power consumption control is the biggest challenge," a senior engineer raised his concern. His name was Li Gong, from a well-known chip foundry with over ten years of ASIC design experience. "If 48 cores run simultaneously, the heat output will be enormous. If thermal management isn't handled properly, the chip could overheat and burn out."

"That's why the thermal design must keep pace," Jiang Xinyu replied. "I plan to use low-power design techniques, adding Dynamic Voltage and Frequency Scaling (DVFS) modules to each core, automatically adjusting frequency and voltage based on load."

"Additionally, we will use QFN packaging, which has a large thermal pad on the bottom that can be directly soldered to the Printed Circuit Board (PCB) for heat dissipation through the PCB."

"Which foundry have you chosen?" another engineer asked.

"SMIC." Jiang Xinyu answered without hesitation. "While their process isn't as advanced as TSMC's, the cost-to-performance ratio is excellent, and communication is more convenient domestically. For a mature process like 130nm, SMIC's quality fully meets our needs."

### I. The Birth of the Iron Flood

Spring 2013. A restless energy hung in the Shenzhen air.

ASICMiner's success was like a stone cast into a lake, the ripples spreading rapidly. The mining of the first block not only validated the technical feasibility—more importantly, it proved the gamble was worth continuing.

Jiang Xinyu stood in BitSpring Company's new office—they had moved out of the original incubator and leased an entire floor of office space.

The sales of the first batch of miners exceeded everyone's expectations. After news spread, orders poured in like snowflakes. Buyers from around the world—from China to the United States, from Russia to Iceland—all scrambling to get their hands on this revolutionary product.

"The first mass production order is confirmed." Wu Jihan walked into the office, carrying a thick stack of contracts. "We need to deliver five thousand miners within three months."

"Five thousand……" Jiang Xinyu frowned. "Can the production capacity keep up?"

"That's exactly what I want to discuss with you." Wu Jihan set the contracts on the desk. "The current contract manufacturing model is too inefficient. We need to establish our own production line."

"Where does the capital come from?"

"Additional financing." Wu Jihan's answer was terse and direct. "The profits from the first batch of miners have proven the business model's viability. Now there are many more investors wanting to get in. Sequoia Capital, IDG, ZhenFund—all have expressed interest. My initial estimate is we can raise another 50 to 100 million RMB."

"Are you sure about continuing to expand?" Jiang Xinyu asked. "Bitcoin price volatility is significant; if the price drops……"

"If the price drops, we ramp up self-operated mining." Wu Jihan cut him off. "Xinyu, you must understand—in this industry, hashrate is power. Whoever controls the hashrate controls the discourse."

"We are not merely selling miners; we are building a hashrate empire."

Jiang Xinyu fell silent. He thought of Satoshi Nakamoto's mention of "one CPU, one vote" in the whitepaper. But reality was clearly veering away from that vision. The emergence of ASIC miners would inevitably drive mining toward centralization.

"Alright." He finally nodded. "But we must control the pace. We cannot sacrifice quality for the sake of expansion."

"Rest assured, quality is your domain—I will not interfere." Wu Jihan laughed. "I'll just handle the money."

---

### II. The Roar of the Factory

May 2013, Dongguan.

BitSpring Company's first production line officially commenced operations in the Dongguan Songshan Lake High-Tech Industrial Development Zone. It was a 5,000-square-meter modern factory floor, equipped with state-of-the-art Surface Mount Technology (SMT) placers and automated testing equipment.

Dressed in anti-static work clothes, Jiang Xinyu stood beside the production line, watching as ASIC chips were precisely soldered onto circuit boards. The SMT machines emitted rhythmic clicking sounds; robotic arms precisely placed tiny components onto the PCBs.

"Production capacity is three hundred units per hour," the factory manager reported to him. "If we run three shifts, monthly capacity can reach two hundred thousand units."

"What about the yield rate?"

"Currently stable at over 97%."

He walked to the testing area, watching workers connect freshly下线 miners to power supplies for burn-in testing. Hundreds of miners ran simultaneously, the roar of cooling fans deafening.

"This is the iron flood." Jiang Xinyu murmured to himself.

In the conference room, Wu Jihan was conducting a video conference with several investors.

"……Yes, our Series B financing is complete, totaling 80 million RMB." Wu Jihan noticed Jiang Xinyu entering and gestured for him to sit. "This round was led by Sequoia Capital, with IDG and ZhenFund participating."

"With this capital, we plan to further expand production capacity and launch the development of the next-generation chip." Wu Jihan continued. "Our goal is to account for over 30% of total network hashrate by year-end."

After the video conference ended, Wu Jihan turned to Jiang Xinyu.

"Did you hear that? 30% of total network hashrate!" he said. "That means we will become the de facto guardians of the Bitcoin network."

"It also means enormous responsibility." Jiang Xinyu responded coolly. "If our hashrate becomes too concentrated, it threatens the network's decentralization characteristics."

"Theoretically, yes. But in reality, competition never stops." Wu Jihan said. "Even if we don't expand, others will. Bitmain, Canaan Creative—they're all circling."

"How is the next-generation chip design progressing?" Wu Jihan asked.

"Already underway." Jiang Xinyu replied. "This time we're using 55nm process, targeting energy efficiency above 1,000 Mhash/J and single-chip hashrate exceeding 10 Ghash/s."

"Excellent. Then let the iron flood keep rolling."

---

### III. The Grandeur of the Mine

Summer 2013, Ordos, Inner Mongolia.

On the vast grasslands, a massive industrial facility was rising. This was the ultra-large mine that BitSpring Company had invested in building—a planned installed capacity of 50 megawatts, capable of accommodating hundreds of thousands of miners running simultaneously.

Jiang Xinyu stood atop a small hill, surveying the construction site before him.

"Why did you choose this location?"

"Three reasons." Wu Jihan held up three fingers. "First, cheap electricity. Ordos's thermal power price is just over two mao per kilowatt-hour—less than one-third of coastal areas. Second, cool climate. The grassland's low average annual temperature can dramatically reduce cooling costs. Third, policy support. The local government has identified the big data industry as a key priority and has offered us numerous incentives."

"When can production begin?"

"The first phase of 10 megawatts can begin operations in September. At full capacity, it can produce over 100 bitcoins per day."

Jiang Xinyu quickly ran the numbers in his mind. At Bitcoin's price at the time, 100 bitcoins were worth approximately $100,000 USD—over $3 million USD per month in revenue.

"But this also means enormous risk." he said. "If Bitcoin's price plummets, or if mining difficulty surges……"

"That's why we need diversification." Wu Jihan cut him off. "In addition to self-operated mining, we must aggressively sell miners. Let others bear the risk of price fluctuations; we earn the sure money."

---

### IV. The Dominion of Hashrate

Autumn 2013. The Bitcoin world ushered in a historic moment.

With the full rollout of ASICMiner miners and the successive commissioning of self-operated mines, the proportion of total network hashrate controlled by BitSpring Company climbed rapidly.

10%…… 20%…… 30%……

By October 2013, this figure reached an astonishing 42%.

This meant that nearly one in every two Bitcoin blocks was mined by ASICMiner's machines. This kind of hashrate dominance was unprecedented in Bitcoin's history.

Discussions about ASICMiner on the Bitcoin forum continued to heat up.

"Friedcat is practically a god! Three months ago, who could have imagined that a crowdfunding project could grow to this scale?"

"42% hashrate…… this is insane. If they wanted to launch a 51% attack, theoretically they could."

"Don't worry, Friedcat isn't that kind of person."

"This is the paradox of decentralization—the technology that pursues decentralization is, in reality, moving toward centralization."

Jiang Xinyu read these discussions, his heart complicated.

He knew that the 42% hashrate share had already touched a dangerous boundary. If it exceeded 50%, the network's bookkeeping rights could theoretically be controlled; the so-called "51% attack" would become possible.

"We should proactively reduce our hashrate share." he proposed at a company meeting.

"What?" Everyone was stunned.

"I said, we should proactively reduce our hashrate share." Jiang Xinyu repeated. "We can pause some self-operated miners, or limit sales of new miners. Keep our share of total network hashrate below 30%."

"Are you insane?" Wu Jihan was the first to object. "Do you know what that means? It means giving up billions in profits!"

"But if I don't, how will the Bitcoin community see us? They'll view us as a threat. In the long run, that's more dangerous than losing some profits."

"I disagree." Wu Jihan said. "Market competition is brutal. If we voluntarily contract, others will fill the void. We'll end up losing both profits and market position."

The two clashed head-on; neither could convince the other.

In the end, the company continued to operate at maximum capacity, with hashrate share hovering around 42% for months.

"Friedcat is becoming the kind of person he once opposed—a monopolist." someone wrote on the forum.

When Jiang Xinyu saw these remarks, his heart was filled with bitterness.

"Power truly does corrupt people." he wrote in his diary. "But what corrupts people more than power is the pressure of maintaining it."

---

### V. The Myth of Wealth

November 2013. Bitcoin's price began an unprecedented surge.

From approximately $200 at the beginning of the month, it broke through $1,000 by month's end—a gain exceeding 400%.

"This month's financial statements are ready." The CFO walked into Jiang Xinyu's office, handing him a document. "You'd better sit down for this."

Jiang Xinyu took the report, his gaze falling on the net profit figure at the bottom—

**230 million RMB.**

His hand trembled slightly.

Three months. In just three months, BitSpring Company's net profit exceeded 200 million RMB.

"The speed of wealth accumulation is enough to suffocate." he wrote in his diary. "But is this money really ours? Or is it merely temporarily entrusted to us?"

"Wu Jihan wants more." he wrote. "He wants to go public, wants to become the world's largest miner manufacturer, wants to build a commercial empire. And me…… I just want to make a perfect chip."

The rift between them was quietly widening.

---

### VI. The Loneliness of the Emperor

December 2013, Shenzhen.

BitSpring Company held a grand annual dinner. In a 5,000-square-meter banquet hall sat the company's senior management, core engineers, investor representatives, and media guests.

Jiang Xinyu stood on stage, accepting the "Annual Leader of China's Blockchain Industry" trophy. Stage lights blazed down on him; applause surged like a tide.

After his speech, he descended the stage and was immediately swarmed by journalists and investors.

"Mr. Friedcat, what is your prediction for Bitcoin's future price?"

"There are rumors that ASICMiner plans to list in Hong Kong—can you confirm?"

Jiang Xinyu answered these questions mechanically, wearing a perfunctory smile.

After the banquet, he drove alone to the seashore.

Shenzhen Bay's night was tranquil and beautiful; Hong Kong's lights glittered across the water. Jiang Xinyu sat on the breakwater, letting the sea breeze wash over him.

"Is this what success feels like?" he asked himself.

42% hashrate share, 200 million RMB in monthly profits, a company valued at over $1 billion. To outsiders, he was already the king of this industry.

But what he felt was a bone-deep loneliness.

His phone rang—it was his wife.

"Xinyu, when are you coming home?"

"Soon. I'll head back after I wrap things up here."

"That's what you always say." Displeasure tinged his wife's voice. "You haven't been home for a month. Our daughter asks about Daddy every day."

Jiang Xinyu fell silent.

"I'm sorry." he said quietly. "I'll definitely come home this week."

After hanging up, he gazed at the lights across the water, his heart full of confusion.

"What am I actually chasing?"

## Part Three: The Pinnacle — 42% Hashrate and the Wealth Myth (2013–2014)

### I. The Throne of Hashrate

January 2014, Beijing.

A conference titled "China Bitcoin Industry Summit" was held at a five-star hotel.

Jiang Xinyu was the keynote speaker of the event. When he walked onto the podium, the entire audience rose to their feet in applause.

"Good morning, everyone." Jiang Xinyu nodded slightly. "Today, what I want to share is not ASICMiner's success story, but some reflections on the industry's future."

He pulled up a slide displaying a global Bitcoin hashrate distribution map.

"As you can see, China's share of global hashrate has exceeded 70%. This is a number we can be proud of, but it also places enormous responsibility on our shoulders."

"Bitcoin's core philosophy is decentralization, but in reality, hashrate is inevitably becoming concentrated. This is no one's fault—it is the inevitable result of technological development. Specialized, large-scale mining will inevitably defeat individual miners. The question is: when hashrate becomes too concentrated, the network's security is threatened."

"So, I solemnly pledge here: ASICMiner will never pursue a hashrate share exceeding 50%. We will proactively limit our own scale, leaving space for other miners and mining pools."

This speech drew warm applause. But deep down, Jiang Xinyu knew this promise would not be easy to keep.

---

### II. The Prelude to a Split

February 2014, Shenzhen.

An acrimonious board meeting was underway in BitSpring Company's conference room.

"I strongly oppose listing in Hong Kong!" Jiang Xinyu slammed the table and rose. "Listing now means handing the company's future over to the capital markets. What do those investors know about technology? They only care about next quarter's earnings report!"

"But the company needs capital to expand!" Wu Jihan retorted. "Competitors are catching up—Bitmain has completed its Series A financing. If we don't accelerate, we'll be overtaken!"

"Then let them overtake me! I want to make the best products, not the biggest company!"

"You're being too idealistic! In this industry, you either move forward or you fall behind. You think technological superiority can keep you ahead forever? Wrong! Channels, brand, capital—these are what determine victory!"

The other directors in the conference room exchanged uneasy glances.

"I propose a vote." Wu Jihan cooled down. "Whether to launch the Hong Kong listing plan."

The voting result chilled Jiang Xinyu's heart—except for himself, nearly every director supported the listing plan.

"You……" He stared in disbelief at these former comrades-in-arms.

"Xinyu," an investor representative spoke up, "we respect your technical ideals, but at this stage of the company's development, we must consider investors' interests. Listing is the best exit route."

"I'm tired." He stood up. "Let's end today's meeting here."

With that, he turned and walked out of the conference room.

---

### III. The Calm Before the Storm

March 2014, Shenzhen.

The spring sun was warm and gentle, but Jiang Xinyu's mood was as cold as ice. Since that board meeting, his relationship with Wu Jihan had plummeted to its lowest point.

Worse, the company's operating conditions had begun to deteriorate. Bitcoin's price, after peaking at $1,000 in December 2013, had been in continuous decline. By March 2014, it had fallen to around $600. Miner sales were severely impacted; many customers cancelled their orders.

"Severe inventory backlog and tight capital chain." The CFO reported to him. "If we can't return capital quickly, the company may face a liquidity crisis."

"Pause the expansion plan and cut operational costs."

A few days later, an article titled "ASICMiner Infighting: The Collision of Technical Ideals and Commercial Reality" circulated online. The article detailed the disagreements between Jiang Xinyu and Wu Jihan, hinting that the company might face a split.

"Who leaked this?" Jiang Xinyu demanded at an emergency meeting.

No one answered. Everyone's eyes quietly glanced toward Wu Jihan.

"Xinyu," Wu Jihan spoke up, "I think you need to take a rest. Your state lately has not been good, and it's had a negative impact on the company's development."

"What do you mean?"

"What I mean is, you should consider taking a temporary leave from the company and adjusting your mindset."

Jiang Xinyu was struck like lightning. He understood—this was Wu Jihan's countermove.

"Fine." Jiang Xinyu gave a hollow laugh. "I'm leaving."

He stood up, removed his employee badge from his chest, and threw it onto the table.

"But I want you all to remember—ASICMiner was founded by me. Without me, you're nothing."

With that, he turned and walked out of the conference room.

---

### IV. The New Mine and the Flash Marriage

April 2014, Sichuan.

After leaving ASICMiner, Jiang Xinyu journeyed alone to Sichuan. This province was China's hydropower powerhouse, and its abundant water resources had attracted numerous Bitcoin mines.

"I don't want to be controlled by others anymore." he told his newly recruited team members. "This time, I will do things my way."

"Our goal is to build a green mine." Jiang Xinyu said at the planning meeting. "Powered entirely by hydropower, achieving carbon neutrality. At the same time, we will develop a new generation of low-power miners, breaking existing efficiency records."

In May 2014, the new mine project officially launched. The site was chosen near a hydroelectric power station in Ganzi Prefecture, Sichuan Province, with a planned installed capacity of 20 megawatts.

Just as the project was in full swing, shocking news stunned the entire team.

Jiang Xinyu was getting married. The bride was a woman named Wang Li; the two had known each other for less than a month.

"Boss, isn't this a bit hasty?"

But Jiang Xinyu appeared calm.

"Maybe." he said. "But I need someone to be with me. I've had enough of the loneliness all these years."

The wedding was simple, with only a few close friends in attendance. The bride, Wang Li, was young and beautiful, gentle and lovely to look at.

## Part Four: The Phantom Vanishes — The Mystery of the Disappearance and the Evaporation of Funds (2014–2015)

### I. The Shadow of the New Mine

June 2014, Ganzi, Sichuan.

Against the backdrop of majestic snow-capped mountains, a modern factory was rising from the earth. This was Jiang Xinyu's new mine—"BitSpring Phase II." Though the name still carried the old brand, it was now a completely independent entity.

"All equipment is in place; trial operations can begin next week." The project manager reported to Jiang Xinyu.

Jiang Xinyu nodded, but his attention seemed elsewhere. Ever since his marriage, his state had not been good; he often sat发呆 in his office alone.

"Mr. Jiang, are you alright?" The manager asked with concern.

"Fine." Jiang Xinyu managed a thin smile. "Just a bit tired."

Wang Li had also come to Sichuan. She owned an apartment in Chengdu and would often return to the city for a few days.

But what he didn't know was that a conspiracy was quietly unfolding.

---

### II. The Evaporation of Funds

July 2014. The new mine officially commenced operations.

The first batch of five thousand miners began running, their enormous power draw making nearby transformers groan in distress.

However, the good times were short-lived.

In early August 2014, the finance manager rushed to Jiang Xinyu with a panicked expression.

"Mr. Jiang, something terrible happened! Most of the funds in the company's account…… are gone!"

Jiang Xinyu was struck numb.

"What? That's impossible!"

"It's true." The finance staff member handed over a bank statement with trembling hands. "Over the past two months, multiple large transfers were made—totaling over 80 million RMB. The recipients were…… personal accounts."

Jiang Xinyu took the statement. His hands shook. He had no knowledge whatsoever of these transfers.

"Where's Wang Li?" It suddenly occurred to him. "Where is she?"

"Ms…… Ms. Wang hasn't come to the company for a week." The finance staff member's expression grew strange. "And…… those receiving accounts seem to be connected to her."

The world spun around Jiang Xinyu.

---

### III. The Wife's Lies

Police investigation quickly yielded results.

Wang Li's identity was false. She was by no means an investor; she was a professional con artist. Her real name was Li Mei, and she had multiple prior convictions for marriage fraud. She specifically targeted wealthy single men, marrying them quickly to steal their assets.

The funds she had embezzled had already been transferred overseas through various channels and were nearly impossible to recover.

"Mr. Jiang, we regret to inform you," the detective in charge said. "The suspect has fled the country, and her current whereabouts are unknown. We are seeking assistance through INTERPOL, but the prospects for recovering the funds are slim."

Jiang Xinyu sat in the police station chair, experiencing a kind of despair he had never felt before.

80 million RMB. That was all the operating capital for the new mine—the hard-earned money that investors had trusted him with.

What was even harder to accept was that he himself had brought the wolf through the door.

"I'm such a fool." he murmured to himself.

---

### IV. Liquidation and Collapse

News of the embezzlement spread rapidly through the industry. Investors came demanding repayment; suppliers demanded immediate payment; the bank froze the company's accounts.

In September 2014, BitSpring Phase II officially declared bankruptcy and entered liquidation. The court seized the mine's equipment; proceeds from the auction were used to repay debts. But the debt hole was far too large—total liabilities exceeded 100 million RMB.

The court ultimately ruled that Jiang Xinyu bore personal joint liability and was required to repay investors' losses exceeding 50 million RMB.

---

### V. The Divorce Verdict

January 2015, Chaoyang District People's Court, Beijing.

Jiang Xinyu stood in the defendant's seat, listening to the judge read the verdict.

"In the case of plaintiff Wang Li (real name Li Mei) suing defendant Jiang Xinyu for divorce…… this court holds that the plaintiff, during the marriage with the defendant, engaged in the act of defrauding the defendant's company funds, constituting a suspected criminal offense. The marriage between the plaintiff and defendant was established on a basis of fraud and shall, in accordance with the law, be determined invalid."

"The verdict is as follows: 1. The marriage between the plaintiff and defendant is declared invalid; 2. The plaintiff's claim for division of property is dismissed; 3. Court costs shall be borne by the plaintiff."

The gavel fell. Silence filled the courtroom.

Jiang Xinyu walked out of the courthouse. The winter sun was pale and harsh. This marriage, lasting less than a year, had ended in the most absurd way possible.

---

### VI. The Final Backside

Spring 2015, Beijing.

Jiang Xinyu stood on the pedestrian walkway in Terminal 3 of the Capital Airport, a simple suitcase behind him. He had bought a one-way ticket to a small, unknown city abroad.

"Are you really leaving?" the friend who had come to see him off asked.

"Yeah." Jiang Xinyu nodded. "There's nothing left for me here."

"What about ASICMiner? Are you going to abandon it?"

Jiang Xinyu forced a bitter smile. ASICMiner had long since had nothing to do with him. After his departure, Wu Jihan had, through a series of financial maneuvers, taken complete control of the company.

"That was no longer my ASICMiner."

He picked up his suitcase and turned toward security.

"Xinyu!" his friend called after him. "Will you ever come back?"

Jiang Xinyu stopped but did not turn around.

"Maybe." His voice was soft, drifting in the wind. "When everything has been forgotten."

Then he continued forward, his figure gradually disappearing into the flow of people.

And so, Friedcat—Jiang Xinyu—the once-formidable father of Bitcoin mining machines, vanished from public view in an almost absurd way.

## Epilogue: The Revival of the Dead (2018, 2024)

### I. The Dormant Addresses

Jiang Xinyu was gone, but his Bitcoin addresses remained on the blockchain. As ASICMiner's founder, he personally held a substantial amount of bitcoin. These coins were distributed across dozens of addresses and had remained dormant since his disappearance.

Within the community, speculation about these addresses' fate was frequent.

"Is Friedcat dead? Otherwise, why haven't these coins moved at all?"

"Maybe he just forgot his private keys."

Days turned to months, months to years. Bitcoin's price went through several bull and bear cycles. The value of the coins in those addresses grew from tens of millions of dollars to hundreds of millions, but no matter how prices fluctuated, these coins never moved.

---

### II. The Anomaly of 2018

January 2018. The Bitcoin community was stunned by news. A long-dormant Bitcoin address, believed to belong to Friedcat, suddenly executed an outbound transaction. The amount was small—just 50 BTC, worth approximately $500,000 at the time.

But the significance of this transaction was extraordinary—it proved that the address's owner, or whoever held the private keys, was still alive.

"Friedcat is alive!" The community erupted.

But the funds were quickly transferred to multiple exchanges, then exchanged for other cryptocurrencies, disappearing without a trace. After that, the address fell silent once again.

---

### III. The Phantom of 2024

March 2024. Bitcoin's price had broken through historical highs, reaching an astonishing $70,000.

Then, something even more staggering occurred. Multiple long-dormant addresses, believed to belong to Friedcat, executed large outbound transfers on the same day. In total, over 10,000 BTC—worth more than $700 million at prevailing prices.

"The dead have revived." someone wrote on the forum.

But as with the 2018 incident, these funds were rapidly dispersed across thousands of different addresses, then, through complex mixing operations, vanished into the vast ocean of anonymity.

No one could trace where the money ultimately went, and no one could locate Friedcat.

---

### IV. The Unsolved Mystery

To this day, the mystery of Friedcat remains unsolved.

Where did he go? Is he alive or dead? Are the movements of those Bitcoin addresses being made by him personally, or by someone who obtained his private keys?

No one knows the answer.

Like Satoshi Nakamoto's disappearance, Friedcat's vanishing has become one of the greatest unsolved mysteries in Bitcoin's history.

## Final Chapter: The Trajectory of a Shooting Star

Friedcat—Jiang Xinyu. This name has left an indelible mark on Bitcoin's history.

He was the father of ASIC mining in China, the first person to transform Bitcoin mining from a hobby into an industry. The ASICMiner he founded once commanded 42% of total network hashrate—a true hashrate hegemon.

But his story is also a tragedy about ideals versus reality, trust versus betrayal, rise and fall.

He trusted too much in technology, yet overlooked the complexity of human nature. He believed that superior chip design alone could secure a place in this world, yet ultimately was devoured by commercial ruthlessness and the darkness of human nature.

He was too lonely, too desperate for warmth—and as a result, fell into a con artist's trap, losing everything.

His disappearance left the world with too many questions and too much to lament.

Perhaps, in some unknown corner, he still lives, watching it all, remembering that night in the Yale laboratory when he first read the Bitcoin whitepaper.

Perhaps he is no longer of this world.

In any case, his story has become part of Bitcoin's legend, a microcosm of that wild era.

A story about genius, wealth, power, and destruction.

A story about a shooting star—burning so brilliantly, vanishing so suddenly.
